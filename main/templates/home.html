FAQs

1. How do I know if I need therapy?

−
If you're feeling overwhelmed, anxious, low, or stuck—or simply want to better understand yourself—therapy can help. You don't need a crisis to benefit from mental health support.

2. What is mental wellness?

+
Mental wellness is a state of well-being in which you realize your own abilities, can cope with normal stresses of life, work productively, and contribute to your community. It encompasses emotional, psychological, and social well-being, affecting how we think, feel, and act in our daily lives.

3. What types of therapy do you offer?

+
We offer various therapeutic approaches including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT), mindfulness-based therapy, family therapy, couples counseling, and individual psychotherapy. Our therapists work with you to determine the best approach for your specific needs and goals.

4. What can I expect in a therapy session?

+
In your first session, your therapist will get to know you and understand your concerns. Subsequent sessions typically involve discussing your thoughts, feelings, and experiences in a safe, confidential environment. Your therapist may introduce coping strategies, exercises, or techniques tailored to your specific needs and therapeutic goals.
